Beth Tellman

Assistant Professor, Geography, Development & Environment

Beth is a postdoc at Columbia University at the Earth Institute, and incoming (Aug 2021) Assistant Professor at the University of Arizona, in the School of Geography, Development, and Environment, and is the co-founder and Chief Science Officer at Cloud to Street. At Columbia, she is mentored by Dr. Upmanu Lall at The Columbia Water Center and Dr. Dan Osgood from the Financial Instrument Sector Team at International Research Institute for Climate and Society. She completed her PhD, advised by Dr. B.L. Turner II and Dr. Dr. Hallie Eakin, in 2019 in the School of Geographical Sciences at Arizona State Unviersity. Her research seeks to address the causes and consequences of global environmental change for vulnerable populations, with a focus on illicit activities, flood risk, water scarcity, and land use change.

Prospective graduate students

I admit graduate students through the School of Geography starting August 2021. Applications are due January 5. Interested students should email Beth a CV, a piece of writing they are proud of, and a paragraph about their interests.

Prospective post-docs

Please be in touch if you are interested in applying to a post-doctoral fellowship to work together. Some potential funding sources for post-docs are the SESYNC post-doc, the various NSF post-docs. I have some funding to support a post-doctoral researcher, if our interests align. Please send an email with your expression of interests and goals, and a published first authored paper.
